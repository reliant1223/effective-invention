    Resume Building (ATS-Friendly)

    1. Highlight Transferable Skills: Use corporate terms for leadership, strategic planning, team management, and risk assessment.
    2. Use Corporate Job Titles: Align with roles such as Operations Manager, Security Administrator, or Project Manager for ATS clarity.
    3. Add Relevant Keywords: Include industry terms like "Project Management," "Supply Chain Management," and "Security Operations."
    4. Show Achievements with Numbers: Use metrics like cost savings, team size, or project timelines to demonstrate impact.
    5. Use Bullet Points: Make responsibilities and achievements clear and scannable for recruiters and ATS.
    6. List Certifications: Include relevant ones like PMP, Six Sigma, or training aligned with corporate needs.
    7. Professional Summary: Start with a brief summary tailored to the role and highlight top skills and goals.
    8. Core Competencies: Add a section for key skills such as "Leadership," "Crisis Management," and "Logistics."
    9. Keep Formatting Simple: Use clean, ATS-friendly fonts and avoid special characters for better readability.

    Why Does an ATS-Friendly CV Matter?
    ATS filters resumes based on keywords and formatting. An optimized resume ensures your application reaches recruiters.
